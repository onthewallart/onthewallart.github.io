MOM'S GALLERY — quick guide (no rush, do this whenever)

TO SEE IT:  double-click  index.html  (opens in your web browser)

WHEN YOU'RE READY TO FILL IT IN — just 3 things:

1) ADD PHOTOS
   - Put painting photos in this "paintings" folder.
   - Name them simply: 01.jpg, 02.jpg, 03.jpg ...
   - In index.html, each painting has a line like:
        <div class="ph">Add painting photo<span>paintings / 01.jpg</span></div>
     Replace that whole line with:
        <img src="paintings/01.jpg" alt="Title">

2) HER NAME + EMAIL
   - Search index.html for [Artist Name] and change it.
   - Change  email@example.com  to her real email (2 spots).

3) TITLES + PRICES
   - Edit the title / medium / size / price text under each painting.
   - For a sold piece, mark it "Sold" (template shows how).

OR — just hand it back to Claude with the photos in this folder and
say "wire up mom's gallery," and it'll do all of the above for you.

LATER: when it's ready, Claude can publish it online as a real
website with its own link she can share.

----------------------------------------------------
SELLING PRINTS (already wired in — almost no work)
----------------------------------------------------
The gallery already has a "Buy Prints" button under every painting.
You just need a print-on-demand account so someone else does the
printing, shipping, and payment. Recommended for paintings:

  FINE ART AMERICA  (pixels.com)  — free to join, made for painters.
  It prints paper / canvas / framed art, ships it, takes the payment,
  and pays her out. She sets her own prices.

SET IT UP (15 min, do it whenever):
  1) Make a free account at pixels.com (Fine Art America).
  2) Upload photos of her paintings; set print prices.
  3) Copy her storefront link (looks like
     https://pixels.com/profiles/her-name ).
  4) In index.html, find:  var STORE_URL = "https://pixels.com";
     and paste her link in place of https://pixels.com.

  That's it — every "Buy Prints" button now sends buyers to her shop.

  (Optional: to point one painting at its own print page, add
   data-print="https://...its-page..."  inside that painting's
   <div class="plaque">  tag.)

Prefer her OWN store instead of a marketplace? Tell Claude and it can
set it up with Gelato + a simple storefront later.
